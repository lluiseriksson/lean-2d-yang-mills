\section{Relation to the programme's strong-coupling area law}

A previous component of the Eriksson programme established a machine-checked,
volume-uniform Wilson-loop area-law bound for $SU(N_c)$ lattice gauge theory in
an explicit strong-coupling regime, using a formalized cluster expansion and a
Koteck\'y--Preiss convergence criterion.  The present work addresses a
mathematically distinct result: the exact heat-kernel solution of
two-dimensional $SU(2)$ Yang--Mills theory.  Its objective is not another
strong-coupling decay estimate, but an unconditional formal chain from Haar and
Weyl integration through character convolution and Migdal subdivision
invariance to the exact simple-loop identity
\[
  \mathbb{E}[W_j(C)]
  = \exp\!\left[-C_2(j)\operatorname{Area}(C)\right].
\]
Subject to a final systematic literature review, the intended novelty claim is
the first end-to-end machine formalization of this exact two-dimensional
heat-kernel/Migdal solution.

The two results differ in both logical form and mechanism.  The earlier theorem
is a perturbative inequality with strictly positive tension in an explicit
strong-coupling window, obtained through polymer and cluster control.  The
present target is an exact identity in the two-dimensional heat-kernel model,
obtained through convolution, edge integration, and subdivision consistency.
Consequently, the earlier theorem is cited as prior work but is not used to
instantiate the exact area-law package of the present development.
